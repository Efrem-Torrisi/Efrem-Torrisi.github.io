The Misadventures of Captain Hornswaggle

A swashbuckling adventure game

Role: Technical Artist Team Size: 17 Engine: Custom DX11 Engine (TGE) Year: 2025

My Contributions

Cel Shading & Outline Shader

Authored the HLSL shaders that define the game's signature stylised look, together with my discipline partner Dawid Batkowski. The cel shading works by quantizing the standard PBR lighting model into discrete color bands, with stylised control over specular highlight color, size and shape. The outline shader runs edge detection on both the depth and normal buffers to catch silhouettes as well as surface detail edges

Post-Processing Shaders

Built additional HLSL post-process shaders to complement the cel shaded look. The depth of field effect samples the scene color texture at increasing mip levels based on depth from the camera to control the blur falloff. Combined with a simple vignette shader, these effects help guide the player's eye toward the gameplay area and give the image a more polished, cinematic feel.

Obstacle Animations

Created animations for the game's obstacles, including the tentacle and cannon hazards as well as the skeleton and pirate enemies. These were rigged, skinned and animated by me in Blender.

Vertex Shader Animations

Wrote vertex shader animations for the kraken tentacles gripping the ship and the surrounding environmental elements. The tentacles pulsate and shift, rocking nearby objects back and forth to sell the feeling of an active kraken attack rather than a static environment.

VFX Flipbooks & Flipbook Shader

Created VFX flipbook spritesheets for the player jump, cannon shooting, cannon ball projectile and explosion effects. I first wrote the flipbook playback logic as a GPU shader, then later the logic was ported to CPU by the programming team so that they could more easily integrate the effects into gameplay systems.
