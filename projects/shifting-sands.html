Shifting Sands

A Souls-like boss rush arena game

Role: Technical Artist, 3D Artist Team Size: ~ 10 Engine: Unity Year: 2023

My Contributions

I joined the team after the initial vertical slice was completed. My role was to strengthen the areas the project was lacking in: shaders, materials, and detailed 3D art. The trailer above is from the original vertical slice, while the work shown below reflects the improvements I made after joining.

Sand Shader & Material

Created the sand shader and material used across the arena environment. The shader includes a dynamic displacement system written with custom HLSL snippets in Unity that deforms the sand surface in real time as objects interact with it, visible in the demo where objects press into and pass through the sand, leaving deformation trails behind them. The displacement is captured using a render texture camera that tracks the interaction area from above.

3D Art & Texturing

Modeled and textured weapons for the game, including the main character's sword.

Outline Shader

During development, the team considered porting the project from Unity to Unreal Engine 5. Since I was already familiar with UE5, I built an outline shader demo to test whether the game's visual direction could translate well to the new engine. Ultimately the team decided to stay on Unity, but the exploration helped inform that decision.

Sand FX Prototypes

As part of the same UE5 exploration, I prototyped sand-related shader effects to test how environmental FX could look in the new engine. The sand here also uses a tri-planar projection shader, which was necessary since the arena geometry is constantly shifting and deforming - standard UV mapping would stretch and break, so tri-planar projection keeps the tileable sand texture looking correct regardless of surface orientation.
